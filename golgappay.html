Top 5 Gol Gappay


Panipuri or Phuchka is a type of snack that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It consists of a round or ball-shaped, hollow puri, filled with a mixture of flavored water, tamarind chutney, chili, chaat masala, potato, onion or chickpeas



1.Chandni Chatkhara


Chandni Chatkhara is a recent entrant in the packed chaat house scene of Karachi and its one of the best places to get your fix. The best part is that it’s in multiple locations from Tipu Sultan Road, Badar Commercial (Defence Phase V), to the food court of Clifton’s Dolmen Mall. The food is hygienic and also quite filling. It can even be a decent place to go for dinner if you don’t feel like having something heavy.

2.Gazebo


You’ll be greeted by the nice lady who owns the place and that will serve as a start to elaborate experience you’ll have at Gazebo. The food is hygienic and the options are plenty. You can even get Masala Dosas and similar South Indian specialties. For chaat, do try the Mixed Chaat Special and Sev Puri and you’ll want another helping as soon as you’re done.

Chatkharay is a Khadda Market chaat and Gol Gappay House joint that serves mouth-watering bun kababs. Their desi burgers are simple, fresh and just oh-so-satisfying.

4.Flamingo Boat Basin


Boat Basin is Clifton’s oldest food strip and Flamingo is the place to go for your favorite evening snack. It’s great for chaat and Gol Gappay but it even serves rolls too. So, you can have a nice chicken roll to go along with your chaat.

5.Bombay Chowpatty


Bombay Chowpatty has become a popular chaat option. It has a proper sit-down restaurant in Zamzama and you can even find a stall at the Clifton Dolmen Mall. Like other such places on the list, it’s comfortable for families and can also double as a place to take your bae on a sasti date.
